Post-doctoral research fellowship in applied statistics

Project description

RESILIENCE aims to exploit simulations from Convection-Permitting Models and novel statistical methods to develop an integrated methodology to assess the impact of climatic variations and changes on the intense precipitation and wind regimes, and on the ensuing triggering of flash floods, debris flows and wind-related forest damages. The focus of the Project is on the Italian North-East. RESILIENCE is structured on four Work Packages (WPs) and brings together an interdisciplinary group of scientists, from hydrologists, to climate modelers, to statisticians, to forest science experts. Within this structure, a WP based at the Department of Statistical Sciences of the University of Padova will focus on novel statistical tools for extreme value modelling to support RESILIENCE activities. Goals of this WP include: development of robust probabilistic model of precipitation and storm wind velocities, and study and development of novel Bayesian methods for extreme value analysis.

Candidate profile

The successful candidate will be a highly motivated researcher with interdisciplinary interests and a strong background in statistical modelling and/or hydro-meteorology. Specifically, candidates holding a PhD in Statistics with a strong background in environmental applications or candidates holding a PhD in Hydrology, Atmospheric sciences, Environmental sciences and related areas with a strong background in stochastic modelling will be preferred. Other qualifications include significant programming skills and research abilities demonstrated by publications. A background in any area of Bayesian statistics is desirable, though not essential. The successful candidate will be based at the Department of Statistical Sciences of the University of Padova and will collaborate with the Institute of Atmospheric Sciences and Climate of the National Research Council of Italy (CNR-ISAC). The candidate is expected to actively participate in the activities of the RESILIENCE team and with the life of the Department of Statistical Sciences.
